### Terminology
> [!summary] Scalar vs Vector
> Scalar values are just "static" numbers. These are usually indicated by letters of the [[Greek Alphabet]]. Vectors, on the other hand, are collections of numbers that represent something and are usually indicated by Latin alphabet, sometimes with an arrow above

### Operations
- **Vector length**:
$$
|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{ x^2 + y^2 + \dots}
$$
- **Unit vector (Einheitsvektor)**:
$$
\hat{v} = \frac{\vec{v}}{|\vec{v}|}
$$
- **Other stuff**
$$
\begin{align}
& 1. \ \lambda \cdot \vec{0} = \vec{0}, \quad \forall \lambda \in K \\
& 2. \ 0 \cdot \nu = \vec{0}, \quad \forall \nu \in V \\
& 3. \ -1 \cdot \nu = -\nu, \quad  \forall \nu \in K
\end{align}
$$
