### Operations
Matrix addition is associative and commutative
Matrix multiplication is associative but distributive in a special way:
$$
\begin{align}
 & A(B \cdot C) = AB \cdot AC \\
 & (B \cdot C)A = BA \cdot CA
\end{align}
$$
Scalar multiplication works pretty standard
However it is not commutative! So $AB \neq BA$
Inverse changes the order: $(AB)^{-1} = B^{-1}A^{-1}$
$$
\det(A^{-1} ) = \frac{1}{\det (A)}
$$
