Proposed in #history/1799 by Paolo Ruffini (who partially proved it), later proved fully in #history/1824 by [[Evariste Galois]] this theory states that no polynomial of higher or equal to the fifth degree (i.e. has $x^{n}$, where $n \geq 5$) has a standard formula through which its roots can be derived using $+, \ -, \ \times, \ \div$ or roots. This is based on the fact that the [[Permutation Group]]s $S_{n}$, where $n \geq 5$ are already basic and can't be broken down further

Furthermore, the modern understanding is that a polynomial has to be a [[Galois Group]] in order for it to be solvable **and** $S_{n}$'s $n$ has to be less than $5$
