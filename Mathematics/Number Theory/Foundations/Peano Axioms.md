Described by Giuseppe Peano in the end of 19th century, they describe the set of natural numbers $\mathbb{N}$:
1. $1$ is a natural number
2. Every natural number has a different (from the one you are comparing with) follower that itself is a natural number
3. Different natural numbers have different followers
4. for $M$: $M \subset \mathbb{N} \ | 1 \in M, \ \forall i \in M \implies M = \mathbb{N}, \text{ where } i \text{ is a follower}$

The last axiom is used to prove other stuff (especially [[Mathematical Induction]]) by showing that $1$ is in a set as well as its followers (i.e. it's $\mathbb{N}$)
