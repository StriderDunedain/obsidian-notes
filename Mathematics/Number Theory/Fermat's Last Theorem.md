Formulated by the French mathematician Fermat, it goes like this:
$$
\forall x, y, z \in \mathbb{Z}^{+}, \ n > 2, \ x^{n} + y^{n} \neq z^{n}
$$
He famously wrote it down in #history/1630 a book that he had a proof but it wouldn't fit the margins of this book

It was finally proved true by Andrew Wiles in #history/1994 after working in solitude in hos attic for seven years (although his original proof was wrong, a year later he and his colleagues corrected it)
