> [!abstract] Idea
> Let $a, \ b$ be integers with a GCD[^1] $d$. Then there exist integers $x, \ y$ such that $ax + by = d$. Moreover, the integers of the form $az + bt$ are exactly the multiples of $d$

$a, \ b$ are called *Bézout coefficients* and are used in [[Extended Euclidean Algorithm]] and [[Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT)]], for example

[^1]: More on that in [[Modulus]]
