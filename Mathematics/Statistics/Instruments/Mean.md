> Answers: "What is the average?"

#### Mean | Discrete Series
The formula for the mean of discrete series is:
$$
\overline{m} = \frac{\sum( x_{i} \cdot f_{i})}{\sum f_{i}}
$$
Which means: in the numerator is the weighted sum – element $x_{i}$ multiplied by its frequency $f_{i}$ – and denominator is essentially the number of elements like in the normal mean but expressed through the sum of frequencies

#### Mean | Ranged series
The formula for ranged series is what people understand as the average: the sum of elements divided by how many of them there are

#### Mean | Interval series
In the interval series the mean is found using this weighted mean:
$$
\overline{m} \approx \frac{\sum (m_{i} \cdot f_{i})}{\sum f_{i}}
$$
Where:
- $m_{i}$ is the mean of the interval (i.e. $100-150 = 125$)
- $f_{i}$ is the same old frequency
