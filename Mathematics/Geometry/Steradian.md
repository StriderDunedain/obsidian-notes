A steradian is a [[SI]] unit. The only one being essentially just a ratio of an area to an area and hence having no special name for itself, still persists and should not be regarded as a ration. Rather it's used for measuring [[Solid Angle]]s (essentially a 3D version of a [[Planar Angle]]). It's calculated using the following formula:

*Ω of 1 sr = A / r² = 2πrh / r*, where

A — the area of the "spherical cap" which, though some strange manner, corresponds to the surface area of a cylinder

Etymology: comes from Greek "stereos" meaning "solid"
