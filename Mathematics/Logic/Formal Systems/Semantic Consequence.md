Known by many different names (like *semantic entailment*) this concept imposes truth unto expressions. Essentially, saying that if the premises are true then the conclusions must also be true. Compare with [[Syntactic Derivation]] and [[Proving Statements in Logic]]
$$
\Gamma \models \phi
$$
"In every [[Model (Math)]] where all formulas in $\Gamma$ are true, $\phi$ is also true"

***
The operator $\models$ is called "models" or "semantic entailment"
The basis for [[Gödel's Completeness Theorem]]
