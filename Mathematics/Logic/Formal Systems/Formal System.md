A formal system is a way of describing different things for the lack of a better word right now that uses, amongst others, these concepts:

### Syntax (form)
> [!faq] What strings of symbols are well-formed?
> This part explains and gives the rules for the structure, the form, of a system without attributing any meaning to it

This includes: alphabets, rules for building formulas (look more into [[Context Free Grammar]]) and definitions of terms and formulas. More in [[Syntactic Derivation]]

### Semantics (meaning)
> [!faq] What do formulas mean when they're true?
> Using syntax tries to establish the veracity and meaning of a [[Model (Math)]] (maybe also of a structure but I'm not sure)

This includes: interpretations (more in [[Structure]]), domains and truth definitions (like $\mathcal{M} \models \phi$)
