A logical deduction, or an inference rule, that states that if the proof of $P$ together with the proof that $P \to Q$ (implies, here) means that $Q$ must also be true. Usual notation is:
$$
\frac{P, \quad P \text{ implies } Q}{Q}
$$
Everything above the line is called *antecedents*. If those prove to be true then everything below the line, the *conclusion* or *consequent*, is also proven true. There're a lot of those, like:
$$
\frac{P \text{ implies } Q, \quad Q \text{ implies } R}{P \text{ implies } R}
$$
or, an interesting one:
$$
\frac{\text{NOT}(P) \text{ implies } \text{NOT}(Q)}{Q \text{ implies P}}
$$
And not the other way round!
