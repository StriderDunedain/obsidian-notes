The opposite of [[Modus Ponens]], this method of proving stuff relies on rejecting the cause when its expected effect doesn't happen. In other words: if $P$ would guarantee $Q$ and $Q$ didn't happen then $P$ cannot be true. Symbolically:
$$
(P \to Q),\ \neg Q \;\vdash\; \neg P
$$
The $\vdash$ is the [[Syntactic Derivation]]
