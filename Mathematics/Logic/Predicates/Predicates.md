These are general statements about something. Like x > y. This is a predicate. $\forall x > y$ is also a predicate

The difference between expressions lies purely in the level of abstraction. Every predicate is an expression but not every expression is a predicate (i.e. 5 > 3 is an expression but not a predicate and 5 > x is an expression and a predicate)
