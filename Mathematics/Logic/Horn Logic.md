Horn logic (introduced by Alfred Horn in #history/1951) is a subset of logic where a clause has a positive term (at most or at least depending on the case, usually at most, though). The basis of [[Logical Programming]]. Same as with [[Resolution]] if there's an $\emptyset$ as one the terms this means that the expression is a contradiction. A very nice consequence of this logic is that every resolvent is itself a horn clause
