Unified Modeling Language was created by ISO in #history/2005 to provide a standard way of modelling processes and behaviors. There are a few types, further described in this [article](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/system-design/unified-modeling-language-uml-introduction/). From a programmatic POV this could be useful to create relationships between classes (which is why this note exists in the first place). Specifically, at time of writing, these are: [[Association (OOP)]], [[Aggregation (OOP)]], [[Composition (OOP)]], [[Dependency (OOP)]] and [[Realization (OOP)]]
