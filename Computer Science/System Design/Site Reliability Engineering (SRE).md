A name given to a few other also nonsensical names that have to do with how well a service is performing (developed in Google):

- ##### Service Level Indicator (SLI)
	A measurement of how well a service is really doing. Not a promise to uphold standards but their general outline and stand as of today, actual numbers

- ##### Service Level Objective (SLO)
	Actual figures that tell something about the system (like availability). This is the stricter version of SLI and is enforced (SLO is the target of SLI). Used to define error budgets (100% - SLO of time to fuck up)

* ##### Service Level Agreement (SLA)
	The shit we tell our customers about how good our system's performance is. Legally binding and is what the customer will receive. Also includes a lot of stuff about what happens if the criteria aren't met, etc.
