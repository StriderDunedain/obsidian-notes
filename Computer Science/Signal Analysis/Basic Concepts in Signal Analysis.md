- Sound pressure, the change in ambient (static) air pressure due to sound, $p$, measured in Pascals
- Sound power, the rate at which sound energy is emitted by a source, $P$, measured in Watts
- Sound intensity, sound power per unit area, $I$, Watts / sq m

### Some proportions
$I \propto p^{2}$
$p \propto \frac{1}{r}$ (sound pressure is inversely proportional to the distance; can be inferred from definitions)
This tells us that is the power $P$ is held constant, the output voltage will decay with $\frac{1}{r}$ distance ratio

Signal amplitude $\propto$ Voltage $\propto$ Sound pressure
