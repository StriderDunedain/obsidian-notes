> [!abstract] Definition
> A signal $x(t)$ is said to be periodic if there exists some finite $t_{0} > 0$ such that for every $t$,
> $$
> x(t + t_{0}) = x(t)
 > $$
 
 That - smallest possible - $t_{0}$ is called the *fundamental period* (or just period)[^1]


### Aperiodic signals
If a signal doesn't have a period, it's called *aperiodic* and has the period of $t_{0} = \infty$ (think of it as waiting literally forever before we see repetition)

[^1]: A close concept is [[Fundamental Frequency]]
