These types of ciphers rely on shifting the letters by some constant $k$ value. The most popular one in the [[Caesar Cipher]]. All of them follow this formula to obtain the ciphertext:
$$
C = (P + k) \ \text{ mod } \text{M}
$$
For notation used in notes, see [[Cryptology Notation]]
